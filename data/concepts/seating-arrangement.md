### Game Concept: Seating Arrangement — Logic Puzzle

This is a deductive reasoning puzzle in which six people must be seated around a 2x3 table according to four written clues. The student drags each person card into a numbered seat and taps CHECK once everyone is placed. It trains careful clue interpretation, relational reasoning (between / across / below / left-of), and spatial placement.

### Core Mechanics & Interface

* **Header Info:** A compact header displays the question number "Q1", a timer "00:02", a star score ("0/10"), and a star progress icon.
* **Clue Box:** The top of the play area shows the four numbered clues that drive the puzzle:
  1. Anu is between Priya and Ravi on one side of the table.
  2. Neha sits across Anu.
  3. Dev sits below Priya.
  4. Neha is on the left of Kiran.
* **Seating Grid:** A bordered **2×3 table** of drop-target cells. **Seat 1, Seat 2, Seat 3** labels sit ABOVE the top-row square drop zones; **Seat 4, Seat 5, Seat 6** labels sit BELOW the bottom-row drop zones. Each drop zone is a plain white square with a thin gray border, and the whole 2×3 area is enclosed in a thin outer black rectangle.
* **Check Button:** A wide **dashed-outline "CHECK" button** (light-gray fill, black dashed border, black text) sits between the seating grid and the character pool.
* **Person Pool:** Below CHECK, six draggable character cards arranged in a **3×2 grid** (top row: **Kiran**, **Ravi**, **Dev**; bottom row: **Anu**, **Priya**, **Neha**). Each card is a white rounded rectangle containing an illustrated avatar with the name typed over it.
* **Progression:** The puzzle runs as a single round. The intro reads, "Use the clues to figure out where everyone is sitting. Drag and drop each person to their seats. Tap check only after you have seated everyone to their correct seats!"

### Behaviors and Interactions

* **Drag-and-Drop (Placement):** The student drags a person card from the pool and drops it into any empty seat. On release, the card snaps into the seat.
* **Check Tap:** Tapping CHECK evaluates the current arrangement against all four clues.

### Victory / Completion

All six seats filled and CHECK tapped with every person in their correct position completes the puzzle.
