### Game Concept: Doubles — Doubling Speed Challenge

This is a rapid-fire mental math game built around doubling. A target number is displayed, and the player selects its double from three multiple-choice options as quickly as possible. The objective is to drill automatic recall of the 2x table so doubling becomes instant.

### Core Mechanics & Interface

* **Status Readouts:** The screen shows a running timer ("00:03"), a score display ("0/10"), and a progress indicator that counts "/15 rounds completed" next to a 3-heart life row and a light-gray progress bar.
* **Question Prompt:** A short line of text reads "Double of" followed by a large target number — on round one, **12**.
* **Options Row:** A single horizontal row of **three rounded-square answer buttons** sit side by side below the target number — white fill, thin gray border — carrying the candidate answers **20**, **28**, and **24** in B1. The layout is a classic "prompt on top / 3-option MCQ row below" — no grid, no extra decoration.
* **Lives Row:** Three **dark (black) heart** icons on the right of the progress bar — note these are dark hearts, not the red hearts used in most other games in this pack.
* **Progression:** The game runs across 15 total rounds. The onboarding prompt emphasizes speed — "Tap on the doubles as fast as possible!" with 3 stars awarded for doubling each number within 2 seconds.
* **Next Round Button:** A yellow rounded-rectangle "Next Round" button anchored bottom-right advances to the next doubling question.

### Behaviors and Interactions

* **Option Tap:** The student taps the button containing the correct double of the target number. The correct answer advances the round; incorrect selections consume a life from the 3-heart counter.
* **Next Round Tap:** Tapping "Next Round" moves the player to the next doubling question once the current one is resolved.

### Variant Progression

All three variants share the "Double of X → pick from 3 options" pattern, 15-round total, 3 lives (black hearts), 10-star target, and the under-2-seconds speed rule; only the concrete target numbers and option triples change across B1/B2/B3 (e.g. B1 opens with 12 and options 20/28/24; B2 with 13 and 26/22/30; B3 with 11 and 26/22/18).

### Victory / Completion

Clearing all 15 rounds completes the game; star count depends on whether the player averaged under 2 seconds per correct answer.
