### Game Concept: Add List with Adjustment Strategy — Mental Math Adjustment

This game teaches the "adjustment strategy" for addition — the idea that a sum becomes easier to compute when one addend is nudged up or down to a benchmark number (like a multiple of 10) and the other addend compensates. The student increments or decrements each number with dedicated buttons, then types the final sum.

### Core Mechanics & Interface

* **Header Status:** Top strip shows a question label "Q1", a timer starting at "00:03", and a star progress readout ("0/10") with a light blue star icon.
* **Round Progress:** A status bar reads "0/9 rounds completed" with 3 black heart lives.
* **Reset Button:** Above and to the right of the workspace sits a dark-gray pill-shaped **"↺ Reset"** button (counter-clockwise arrow glyph + white "Reset" text) that restores both numbers to their starting values.
* **Addition Workspace:** Two vertical "adjust stacks" sit side by side with a small black **`+` operator glyph** between them. Each stack is three stacked cells: a **light-pink tinted `-` button on top**, a **large white number display in the middle** (thin bordered box showing a bold two-digit value), and a **light-green tinted `+` button on the bottom**. Round one of B1 opens with **58 + 72**; B2 opens with **62 + 68** (number box has a light-blue border); B3 opens with **66 + 74**.
* **Answer Field:** Centred below the workspace is a single square input box with a light-gray background and a gray **"?"** placeholder, where the student types the final sum.
* **Progression:** The game is structured as 9 rounds across 3 levels. The intro reads, "Add the numbers using the adjustment strategy. Check the list, think smart, and adjust wisely. Clear all 3 levels with an average speed of less than 15 seconds per level to earn 3 stars."

### Behaviors and Interactions

* **Plus / Minus Tap:** Tapping + or - on either stack increments or decrements that addend by one, letting the student shuffle ones between the two numbers to reach an easier pair (e.g. 58 + 72 → 60 + 70).
* **Reset Tap:** Tapping "Reset" restores both addends to their original values.
* **Answer Input (Type):** The student taps the input field and types in the final sum of the adjusted (or original) numbers.
* **Next Round Tap:** Tapping "Next Round" submits the typed answer and moves to the next problem.

### Variant Progression

All three variants share the +/-/reset/type-sum workspace, the 9-rounds-across-3-levels progression bar, 3 hearts, and the "<15 sec per level" 3-star rule. Only the starting addends change:
* **B1 (canonical, 310474):** 58 + 72.
* **B2 (310481):** 62 + 68 (number boxes have a light-blue border).
* **B3 (310488):** different opening addends but same control layout.
