VoiceInput — Test Cases

Serve this over localhost or https (mic access requires a secure origin). Work through each case; report which ones look/behave wrong.

1 Toolbar padding & layout
Visual check only. Buttons should have comfortable horizontal spacing (matches React's default button padding), mic & keyboard on left, reset pushed to the right.
2 Permission-denied popup
When the user has not granted mic access and taps the big record button, a popup should appear.
  1. Click Force: not permitted (fakes the denied state without needing a real deny).
  2. Tap the mic tool (toolbar). The bottom drawer opens.
  3. Tap the big blue record button. The popup should appear over the dimmed screen.
  4. Click Got it (or the backdrop) to dismiss.
  5. Also try Show popup directly to preview the popup on its own.
3 Loading subtitle during transcription
After you stop recording, the loading audio plays AND the subtitle "wait a sec, converting your speech to text" should appear at the bottom.
  1. Tap the mic tool → tap the big record button → speak a word or two → tap again to stop.
  2. While the spinner/loading audio plays, the subtitle box should be visible.
  3. On success: input is populated, sound_end event fires (see log).
4 Error subtitle + re-focus after 5s
Simulates a failed transcription. You should hear the error sound, see the subtitle "oops, failed to understand your speech…", and the field should auto-refocus 5 seconds later.
  1. Click Simulate error.
  2. Loading subtitle shows briefly → flips to error subtitle.
  3. Wait 5 seconds → focused state returns (border turns yellow, drawer may re-open).
5 Timeout subtitle + re-focus after 5s
Simulates a network timeout. Different sound + different subtitle than Case 4.
  1. Click Simulate timeout.
  2. Subtitle should say "oh no, something went wrong. Please try speaking again…".
  3. Wait 5 seconds → re-focuses.
6 Custom loading audio & subtitle
Instantiated with loadingAudio and loadingSubtitle overrides. When you simulate success, the custom subtitle ("Hang on… processing your voice…") should appear instead of the default.
7 External cancelRecording()
Host code can stop an in-progress recording without a transcription (matches React's emitter.emit("cancelRecording")).
  1. Tap mic → tap the big record button → start recording.
  2. Click Cancel recording. Recording stops, no transcription, no error subtitle.
